Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is the Global Friends Summer Work and Travel Program?
    The Global Friends Summer Work and Travel Program offers university students the chance to work and travel in the United States during their summer break. Participants gain valuable cultural experiences, explore the U.S., and develop professional skills through various job opportunities.
  • Who is eligible to apply?
    Eligible applicants are full-time university students aged 18-23 who are actively pursuing a Bachelor’s degree. Applicants must have Upper-intermediate to Advanced English proficiency to qualify.
  • What documents are required for the application?
    Applicants need to submit a valid travel passport, proof of university enrollment, a recent academic transcript, and a resume. These documents are essential to move forward in the application process.
  • When is the application deadline?
    The application deadline varies each year, typically closing around early December. However, this can change based on the availability of spots, so early submission is encouraged to secure your place.
  • How do I apply for the program?
    You can apply online via our website. The application process includes filling out an online form, after which our team will review your submission. If accepted, you will be contacted for the next steps in the process.
  • What types of jobs are available?
    Participants can choose from a variety of job roles in industries such as hospitality, retail, amusement parks, and restaurants. Positions range from customer service roles to lifeguarding, providing a broad spectrum of opportunities.
  • How long does the program last?
    The program duration is typically 3-4 months, coinciding with your university summer break. This allows you to gain experience and travel without interrupting your studies.
  • Do I get paid for the work?
    Yes, participants are paid according to U.S. wage laws. The hourly pay rate generally ranges from $12 to $17, depending on the job type and location.
  • Will I need a visa to participate?
    Yes, a J-1 Exchange Visitor Visa is required to participate in the program. Global Friends Agency will assist you throughout the visa application process. While we ensure accurate and timely processing, visa approval is not guaranteed as it depends on individual circumstances.
  • Can I participate in the program more than once?
    Yes, students who continue to meet the eligibility criteria can participate in the program multiple times. Each year, you must reapply and fulfill all program requirements.
  • Is accommodation provided?
    Accommodation options vary by employer. Some employers offer housing directly, while others assist in finding suitable accommodations. Typically, housing costs range from $100 to $200 per week, which is usually deducted from your paycheck.
  • Can I travel within the U.S. during the program?
    Yes, participants are allowed to travel within the U.S. during their days off and after their work commitments are completed, as long as it falls within the validity period of your visa.
  • What are the costs associated with the program?
    The program costs include a program fee of $3,250, a visa application fee of $185, airfare (approximately $1,200), and personal expenses (around $1,000). Some employers may offer subsidized housing and meals, which can help reduce costs.
  • What kind of support will I receive while in the U.S.?
    Global Friends Agency provides 24/7 support for all participants. This includes assistance with job placements, emergency support, cultural orientation sessions, and ongoing guidance throughout your stay in the U.S.
  • What does the program fee cover?
    The program fee includes job placement, premium medical insurance, DS-2019 Form and FedEx fees, SEVIS fee, sponsor service fees, and local agency service fees. Additionally, participants receive program orientation in Tashkent, visa preparation, and branded gifts like water bottles, tote bags, and an American SIM card.
  • Is Visa guaranteed?
    No, visa approval is not guaranteed as it is based on individual circumstances. However, we assure timely and accurate processing of all necessary documentation to improve your chances.
  • What happens after the program ends?
    After completing the program, participants must return to their home country to continue their studies. Many alumni stay connected with Global Friends for future opportunities, networking, and alumni events.
  • Are there any extra costs?
    In addition to the primary program costs, participants should budget for personal expenses, travel insurance, and potential housing deposits. Be sure to plan accordingly.
  • Is medical insurance provided?
    Yes, the program fee includes premium medical insurance coverage for the duration of your stay in the U.S. This ensures that participants are protected in case of any medical emergencies.
  • What if I need emergency support while in the U.S.?
    Global Friends Agency offers 24/7 emergency support. Participants can reach out to our support team at any time for help with medical emergencies, housing issues, or other urgent needs.
